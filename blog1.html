Apple and Google Hushed Coronavirus!

In a fraction of second, Coronavirus has spread too quickly for frontline staffs to keep up with traditional methods. Thus, our cellphones have taken charge to help us out of this misery! Tech Giants, Apple and Google have shaken hands to keep us safe and sound. They have announced an expansion of their coronavirus warning software called "exposure notifications express." The software is built into the operating systems on Google's Android phones and Apple's iPhones, and employs Bluetooth to determine if people have spent significant time near each other. For the application to track coronavirus, participating public health agencies provide the person with a unique number when entered into the system for users to receive a pop-up notification. Therefore, if a person tests positive, other participants who've come into contact with that person get notified. It is the software of 2020 because it can save lives! It creates an alert a participant's close contacts if that person tests positive for the virus. They can also share their Bluetooth data and receive alerts when they come in contact with someone diagnosed with the coronavirus. To further enhance the systems, surveillance footage is also used to determine where the diseases might have spread. Health experts and researchers have co-created a wonder to make a difference in the spread of the virus with the help of technology with fewer privacy concerns. It was a relief to a lot of people around the world, but the software got off to a rocky edge when countries such as South Korea and Israel attempted to use technology to tackle the terrifying situation, using cellphone records and GPS data. While trying to track the virus, these methods have also drawn criticism from privacy advocates citing privacy concerns. Therefore, Apple and Google have declined to give public health agencies special access for the purpose of contact tracing.
