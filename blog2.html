Halloween is knocking!

At the last days of the scorching sunny days, as the days get shorter and the nights get longer and colder, I find myself getting excited for the chills this 31st October. Hygge Halloween it is! And, if that sounds a little crazy, then let me share with you why I feel that way. Do you find yourself living for the weekends even though its work from home mostly? I know. Me too. The reason I call myself a Hygge Halloween is because this is the time of the year for self-reflection- Am I the monster or the angel at the dark? It is time for you to look at your personal goals, declutter your mind and hit the reset the button, because life does not have the Undo button. So, I find it exciting enough to pause and look back on my life for the months that I have managed to pass with my demons. I desperately look for a chance to decompress and ignite into my passion. Perhaps you’ve been waiting for a sign to begin the practice of reading to calm the voices inside of your head. Halloween will be upon us and never too late to get your pumpkins and candles ready, so you can dig into the perfect spooky reading time of the year. Maybe you can also curl up with the comfy throw blanket because the weather has a noticeable chill in the air too, So, there is nothing better than starting my top suggestions for what to read this Halloween: The Picture of Dorian Gray Surely, many of know that the story is about q beautiful, young man who indulges in just about every sin you can think of. What the sets the book a little different is that it has a philosophical tough to it. Guilty that I love his hedonistic lifestyle that leads to his death. IT I was intrigued to the novel because the book alternates between two periods of childhood trauma. And, believe it or not most of us have gone through somewhat trauma and that that might have followed us into our adult lives. The film has been a great hit too so if you have not seen it yet, I suggest you grab the book first! Last but not the least… Short Stories and Poems by Edgar Allan Poe If you are not a reader but you want to hit the Hygge Halloween season, this book is for you! This book is just not only gothic but he also writes a lot about death and mourning. What a time to binge this this specific book, you see; a lot of people are taking their last breath due to the pandemic. So why feel their thoughts and be grateful of everything that we still hold today. Calm your demons this Halloween before they start controlling you! Black Butterfly A book my R.M. Drake is the most mesmerizing combination of solitude, falling in love with all the moments and learning nothing lasts forever. I feel, the book is meant to take our breath away by the great fashion artworks to inspire you and that you too can be a glorious mess! These are the perfect books to gab if you did not have time or you were stuck in a rut. Read something exciting or a book that gets you like no other maybe in short bursts.

shh...hygge halloween is coming!
